I. The Lineage, Rewoven

The House began beneath Byzantine light with Dom Reccius di Catanzaro, Dux Recciae, a sixth‑century founder whose descendants later appear in Venetian registers as Signori di Piove di Sacco and in the south as Signori di Monteodorisio and Conti della Scordia【22†House_de_Reccia_de_Cristofaro_Formal_Register】. In the Renaissance, Domenico de’ Medici de Reccia married Donna Isabella de Reccia, joining the Medici current to the Reccian stem — a distant kinship with Queen Catherine de’ Medici that still flickers in the record【23†Royal_and_Dynastic_Ancestry_of_Justin_de_Reccia_de_Cristofaro】.

Across centuries of viceregal oversight and imperial restorations, the House kept its bearings: under Spain’s Habsburg administration in Naples【25†Kingdom_of_Spain_Lineage_House_de_Cristofaro】; through the post‑Napoleonic settlements of the Austrian Empire【28†Austrian_Empire_Lineage_House_de_Cristofaro】; and onward to Malta, where the Cristofaro name bound to the de Piro nobility in 1671【24†Malta_Proof_Sheet】.

III. The Black Inheritance

Every dynasty keeps a room without windows. The private Black Dossier records the shadows alongside the laurels: collateral branches entangled with Fascism; a senator‑kin strained by corruption politics; and Justin’s own documented passage through psychiatric care and self‑destructive weather. The family also reckons with the Medici kinship to the St. Bartholomew’s Day Massacre — history’s ash in the blood【37†Justin_DiCristofaro_Black_Dossier.pdf】.

He does not lacquer the record. Instead, he turns it outward — mental‑health advocacy, candor as discipline, beauty as responsibility.

Modern Vocation
